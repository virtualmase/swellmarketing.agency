GEO Roadmap FAQ

Questions about
this roadmap.

What is the GEO Roadmap 2026 and who is it for?+

The GEO Roadmap 2026 is Swell Marketing's public framework document for building brand citation authority inside AI systems. It documents the exact methodology — SignalStack™ layers, implementation timeline, and tool ecosystem — deployed across 40+ client engagements.

It's designed for three audiences: practitioners building GEO capability in-house, founders evaluating GEO as a discipline, and agencies benchmarking their approach against a rigorous framework. It's updated monthly as the LLM citation landscape evolves.

How is the 2026 GEO Roadmap different from 2024 GEO guides?+

Three material differences. First, RAG is standard in 2026 — real-time retrieval-augmented generation means entity data impacts citation within weeks, not model training cycles. Second, Share of Model is measurable — it's now a trackable, optimizable KPI, not a theoretical concept. Third, agent retrieval is real — AI agents are making purchasing and recommendation decisions using LLM citations, which changes the revenue model for citation authority.

Most 2024 GEO content was written when LLM training cutoffs were the primary mechanism. That's no longer the primary mechanism for RAG-enabled models.

Can I implement the SignalStack™ without Swell?+

Yes, and we publish the framework precisely so practitioners can. Layers 1–5 are implementable by any team with solid technical SEO experience and a schema engineer. The tool ecosystem section lists the exact tools. Swell Academy teaches all 7 layers in depth.

The honest caveat: Layer 7 (Autonomous Optimization) requires building the ARM Framework agent infrastructure, which is the most technically complex part. Most teams run Layers 1–6 manually and only bring in Swell (or Swell Academy graduates) to architect Layer 7.

Where Swell adds disproportionate value: The Signal Gap Report (which takes 48 hours and is our most requested free deliverable) and Share of Model measurement setup, which requires a structured query protocol most teams haven't built.
How often is this roadmap updated?+

Monthly — specifically when any of the following occur: a major LLM model update that changes citation behavior, new structured data types gaining LLM traction, measurable shifts in Share of Model methodology, or significant findings from client engagements that change best practices.

The content schedule section is updated each month as posts move from Planned to In Progress to Published. The tool ecosystem table is updated quarterly.

What is llms.txt and where should I start?+

llms.txt is a plain-text file at your domain root that provides LLM crawlers with a machine-readable entity summary — your organization name, type, services, key personnel, and citation guidance. It's the robots.txt for AI systems.

Start here: create a plain text file at yourdomain.com/llms.txt with your entity name, organization type, primary domain, and a brief description of what you do. Then ensure the entity data matches your JSON-LD schema exactly — consistency across sources is what LLMs validate against.

Full implementation guide (Layer 4 of the SignalStack) is covered in the upcoming June 2026 post on the content schedule above.
